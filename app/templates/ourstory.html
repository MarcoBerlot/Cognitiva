Idea Density

When we write, we aim to express our thoughts in a clear and intelligent manner. But how can we measure if we are succeeding in doing so?

One’s Idea Density refers to how concisely they express themselves in their writing. Specifically, it refers to the number of “ideas” they express every 10 words, where ideas correspond to elementary propositions (typically verbs, adjectives, adverbs, or prepositional phrases).

In 1996, a groundbreaking study was published that showed that our idea density might tell us more than just about our writing patterns. This “Nun Study” tracked a group of Nuns over time, and showed that the idea density in their writing in their early lives could foretell their risk of developing Alzheimer’s decades later.

We believe that Idea Density may be a key to unlock secrets about Alzheimer’s so that we can predict it earlier and better understand how to prevent it. By contributing your data, you are helping advance research to understand this powerful concept.
